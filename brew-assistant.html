        Quantify your brew with refractometer and dose data.

        What you're measuring

        TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) is the percentage of your beverage that is dissolved coffee compounds — measured with a refractometer. A reading of 1.35% means 1.35 g of solids per 100 g of liquid.

        Extraction Yield tells you what fraction of the coffee's dry mass ended up in the cup. EY% = TDS% × beverage mass ÷ coffee dose. The SCA target for filter coffee is 18–22%.

        Using a refractometer

        01 Let your brew cool to room temperature (or at least below 45 °C) before sampling. Heat changes the refractive index and skews readings.
        02 Calibrate with distilled water first — the reading should be 0.00%. Temperature-compensating (ATC) models handle this automatically.
        03 Place 2–3 drops on the prism. Close the cover, wait 30 s, then read. Coffee refractometers read in Brix — multiply by 0.85 for approximate TDS%.
        04 Take 2–3 readings and average them. A dedicated coffee refractometer (VST, Atago) gives direct TDS% without conversion.
